# Introduction: The Quest to Give Machines a Mind

When you turn on a computer for the first time, the interface that appears on the screen is the product of decades of struggle — the glow of monitors lighting up the nights of geniuses, frustrations, and moments of triumph. The operating system — the OS — is like the "soul" of a computer. Hardware cannot run without software; but it is the operating system that bridges software and hardware, making them meaningful.

The history of operating systems is also a story that transformed how humanity thinks. This journey, stretching from switches clicking in dark laboratories to touchscreens dancing at fingertips, is an epic of engineers, visionaries, and sometimes serendipitous discoveries.

> *This article aims to tell the historical arc of operating systems — from their birth to the present — together with pivotal turning points, unforgettable stories, and the human dimension behind them.*
