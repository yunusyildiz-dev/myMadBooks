# Part 5: The Internet Age — 1990s

## Windows 95 and the Internet Wave

Windows 95 was not just an operating system — it was a cultural phenomenon. The Start menu, the taskbar, long file names, and especially internet connectivity support bundled with Internet Explorer... 7 million copies sold in the first few weeks of 1995.

However, the integration of Internet Explorer into Windows simultaneously triggered one of history's greatest antitrust cases. The US Department of Justice alleged that Microsoft abused its market power to crush competing browsers. This case, which extended into the 2000s, permanently shaped regulatory approaches toward technology monopolies.

🔗 [United States v. Microsoft Corp.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_v._Microsoft_Corp.)

## Apple's Dark Period and Jobs's Return

In 1985, Steve Jobs had been forced out of the very company he had founded. For 12 years, Apple declined. In 1997, when the company was on the brink of bankruptcy, Jobs returned — and he brought a treasure with him: the [NeXTSTEP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NeXTSTEP) operating system he had developed at NeXT Computer.

NeXTSTEP was Unix-based, object-oriented, and supremely elegant. This system became the foundation of Mac OS X. After Jobs returned, iMac, iPod, iPad, iPhone... each a revolution in its own field — and at the heart of each, this solid and elegant system nourished by the Unix legacy.
