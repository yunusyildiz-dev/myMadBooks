# Part 8: Turkey and Operating Systems

## Pardus: A National Experiment

In 2003, Turkey began developing a national Linux distribution called [Pardus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pardus_(operating_system)) under the coordination of TÜBİTAK. Debian-based, Pardus was designed particularly for use in public institutions and attracted international attention.

Pardus contained technically innovative features with its original package manager **PiSi** and service management system **COMAR**. However, with the transition to a new Debian-based structure in 2012, the project lost its pace of original development. Nevertheless, it continues to carry symbolic value in Turkey's debates about software sovereignty.
